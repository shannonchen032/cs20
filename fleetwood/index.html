Fleetwood Mac's.
Five bandmates.
Three tangled hearts.
Mick Fleetwood   loved  Stevie Nicks.
Mick Fleetwood — drummer and founding heartbeat of Fleetwood Mac — fell for Stevie Nicks during the band’s peak. Their brief affair, tangled in secrecy, reflected the chaos surrounding the making of Tusk (1979).
Although Mick wasn’t one of Fleetwood Mac’s primary songwriters—and he never wrote songs about Stevie — their personal entanglements still left a mark. When Mick began dating Stevie’s best friend, Sara Recor, it shattered her. Out of that heartbreak came Sara ▶
But before Mick Fleetwood, before Sara, there was the great love story with Lindsey Buckingham
Their relationship began long before Fleetwood Mac. But fame, resentment, and control eroded the love they once had. Still, they kept writing—and singing—to each other, even as they fell apart.
Stevie answered Lindsey with Dreams ▶ — her aching truth.
And Lindsey loved the band more.
When their relationship ended, he didn’t leave. He stayed. He buried the heartbreak and channeled it into the music — but the pain still bled through the chords he played.
In Go Your Own Way ▶ he lashed out—frustrated and exposed.
Then in Second Hand News ▶ , he masked grief with rhythm, but the message cut deep.
The heartbreak, the tangled love triangle—
it all lived on in the music.
And 22 years later, the wound still hadn’t closed.
Stevie answered again—this time onstage,
face to face
with Silver Springs in 1997.
